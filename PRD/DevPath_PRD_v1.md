# DevPath Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## 1. Vision

DevPath aims to become the career navigation platform for Computer Science students.

Our vision is to eliminate the confusion and uncertainty students face while preparing for technical careers by helping them understand where they currently stand, where they want to go, and what their next best step should be.

Instead of following generic roadmaps, random online advice, or scattered resources, every student receives a personalized learning journey based on their current skills, career goals, and available time.

## 2. Problem Statement

Computer Science students today have access to unlimited learning resources including YouTube, online courses, AI tools, blogs, documentation, and community platforms. While information has become more accessible than ever, choosing the right path has become increasingly difficult.

Students often struggle to answer questions like:

- Where do I currently stand?
- What should I learn next?
- Which roadmap is right for me?
- Am I ready for internships?
- Am I wasting time learning the wrong things?

This lack of personalized guidance often leads to confusion, decision paralysis, inconsistent learning, and slower career growth.

DevPath solves this problem by helping students make confident learning decisions through personalized career journeys that evolve with their progress.

## 3. Product Principles

DevPath follows five core principles throughout the product.

### 1. Clarity over Complexity

Every screen should reduce confusion instead of adding more information.

### 2. Personalized Guidance

Every student should receive guidance based on their own skills, goals, and progress instead of generic roadmaps.

### 3. Evidence over Assumptions

Progress should be earned by demonstrating skills rather than simply consuming content.

### 4. Progress should feel rewarding

Students should always feel a sense of achievement after completing meaningful milestones.

### 5. Every interaction should answer one question:

"What should I do next?"
